Eligibility Criteria

Description of the Course

The Course for the Full Time Diploma in Engineering shall extend over a period of three academic years, consisting of 6 semesters* and the First Year is common to all Engineering Branches. Each Semester will have 16 weeks duration of study with 35 hrs. / Week for Regular Diploma Course.

Condition for Admission

Condition for admission to the Diploma courses shall be required to have passed in The S.S.L.C Examination of the Board of Secondary Education, Tamil Nadu.(Or) The Anglo Indian High School Examination with eligibility for Higher Secondary Course in Tamil Nadu.(Or) The Matriculation Examination of Tamil Nadu.(Or) Any other Examinations recognized as equivalent to the above by the Board of Secondary Education, Tamil Nadu.
Note: In addition, at the time of admission the candidate will have to satisfy certain minimum requirements, which may be prescribed from time to time.

Admission to Second year (Lateral Entry)

  • A pass in HSC (academic) or (vocational) courses mentioned in the Higher Secondary Schools in Tamil Nadu affiliated to the Tamil Nadu Higher Secondary Board with eligibility for university Courses of study or equivalent examination &Should have studied the following subjects. A pass in 2 Years ITI with appropriate Trade or Equivalent examination.
  • For the Diploma Courses related with Engineering/Technology, the related / equivalent subjects prescribed along with Practicals may also be taken for arriving the eligibility.
  • Branch will be allotted according to merit through counseling by the respective Principal as per communal reservation.
  • Candidates who have studied Commerce Subjects are not eligible for Engineering Diploma Courses.

Age Limit

No Age limit

Medium of Instruction

English

Eligibility for the Award of Diploma

No candidate shall be eligible for the Diploma unless he/she has undergone the prescribed course of study for a period of not less than 3 academic years in any institution affiliated to the State Board of Technical Education and Training, Tamil Nadu, when joined in First Year and two years if joined under Lateral Entry scheme in the second year and passed the prescribed examination. The minimum and maximum period for completion of Diploma Courses are as given below:

Full Time

Minimum Period = 3 Years & Maximum Period = 6 Years

Full Time (Lateral Entry)

Minimum Period = 2 Years & Maximum Period = 5 Years

This will come into effect from N Scheme onwards i.e. from the academic year 2020- 2021

Subjects of Study and Curriculum outline

The subjects of study shall be in accordance with the syllabus prescribed from time to time, both in theory and practical subjects.

Scholarship

A scholarship is a form of financial aid that is awarded to a student to help cover the costs of their education. Scholarships are typically awarded based on academic merit, financial need, or a combination of both. They can be awarded by colleges and universities, private organizations, corporations, or government agencies. Scholarships can be awarded for a variety of reasons, including academic achievement, athletic ability, community service, or specific areas of study. They can cover a range of expenses, including tuition, fees, room and board, textbooks, and other educational expenses. Unlike student loans, scholarships do not have to be repaid. This makes them an attractive option for students who need financial assistance but do not want to accumulate debt. In addition to providing financial assistance, scholarships can also help students gain recognition for their achievements and enhance their resumes. Here are some popular scholarships for college students:

PRAGATI SCHOLARSHIP SCHEME FOR GIRL STUDENTS (TECHNICAL DIPLOMA)

PRAGATI (Providing Assistance for Girls' Advancement in Technical Education) is a scholarship scheme offered by the Government of India for girl students pursuing technical education at the diploma level. The objective of this scheme is to encourage more girls to take up technical education and provide them with financial assistance to pursue their studies. Under the PRAGATI scheme, girl students who have taken admission in the first year of a diploma programme in AICTE (All India Council for Technical Education) approved institutions can apply for the scholarship. The scheme provides financial assistance to cover the cost of tuition fees, examination fees, books, and other incidental expenses. The scholarship is only available to girls who have a family income of less than Rs. 8 lakh per annum. The amount of the scholarship is Rs. 50,000 per annum for a maximum period of three years or the duration of the course, whichever is less. To apply for the PRAGATI scholarship scheme, eligible students need to visit the National Scholarship Portal (NSP) and register themselves. After registration, they can apply for the PRAGATI scholarship scheme by filling out the application form and uploading the necessary documents. The selection of candidates for the scholarship is based on the merit list of the previous qualifying examination. Candidates are advised to visit the official website of the AICTE or the NSP for more details on the eligibility criteria and application process for the PRAGATI scholarship scheme.

Post Matric Scholarships Scheme for Minorities

The Post Matric Scholarship Scheme for Minorities is a financial assistance scheme for students belonging to minority communities in India who are pursuing post-secondary education. The scheme is implemented by the Ministry of Minority Affairs, Government of India, and aims to provide financial assistance to students from minority communities to help them pursue their studies and improve their employment opportunities. Under the scheme, students belonging to minority communities such as Muslims, Christians, Sikhs, Buddhists, Jains, and Zoroastrians can apply for the scholarship. The scholarship is available for students studying in Class 11 to Ph.D. level courses in government or private institutions. The scholarship covers the cost of tuition fees, maintenance allowances, and other incidental expenses. The amount of the scholarship varies according to the course level and the category of the student. The scholarship is disbursed directly to the bank account of the student through Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT). To be eligible for the scholarship, students must belong to a minority community, have a family income of less than Rs. 2 lakh per annum, and have secured at least 50% marks or equivalent in the previous qualifying examination. To apply for the scholarship, eligible students need to visit the National Scholarship Portal (NSP) and register themselves. After registration, they can apply for the scholarship by filling out the application form and uploading the necessary documents. Students are advised to visit the official website of the Ministry of Minority Affairs or the NSP for more details on the eligibility criteria and application process for the Post Matric Scholarship Scheme for Minorities.

CENTRAL SECTOR SCHEME OF SCHOLARSHIPS FOR COLLEGE AND UNIVERSITY STUDENTS

The Central Sector Scheme of Scholarships for College and University Students is a merit-based scholarship scheme implemented by the Ministry of Education, Government of India, for students pursuing undergraduate and postgraduate courses in recognized institutions in India. The scholarship aims to support financially disadvantaged students who have a good academic record and provide them with the opportunity to pursue higher education and realize their potential. Under the scheme, scholarships are awarded to students who have scored at least 80% marks in the previous qualifying examination. The scholarship is available for students pursuing regular full-time courses in arts, science, commerce, engineering, medicine, and other professional courses. The scholarship covers the cost of tuition fees, maintenance allowances, and other incidental expenses. The amount of the scholarship is Rs. 10,000 per annum for the first three years of undergraduate courses, and Rs. 20,000 per annum for postgraduate courses. The scholarship is disbursed directly to the bank account of the student through Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT). To be eligible for the scholarship, students must be Indian nationals, have scored at least 80% marks in the previous qualifying examination, and come from families with a gross annual income of less than Rs. 8 lakh. The application process for the scholarship is online through the National Scholarship Portal (NSP). Eligible students can apply for the scholarship by registering themselves on the NSP and filling out the application form with the necessary details and documents. Students are advised to visit the official website of the Ministry of Education or the NSP for more details on the eligibility criteria and application process for the Central Sector Scheme of Scholarships for College and University Students.
